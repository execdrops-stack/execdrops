# EXEC Q2 Plan

## Objective
Turn EXEC from a novelty launch into a functioning, profitable micro-brand.

## Primary Q2 goals

1. Sell through Drop 001 or get clear evidence of demand
2. Build a repeatable content engine around the story, not just the shirt
3. Capture audience data, especially email, for Drop 002
4. Establish weekly operating metrics so decisions are based on signal, not vibes

## What EXEC actually sells
EXEC sells a story about machine authorship, alignment, and early adoption.
The shirt is the artifact.
The content is the top of funnel.
The registry language is the retention mechanism.

## Q2 priorities

### Priority 1: Move Drop 001
- Post 3 to 5 times per week on TikTok and Instagram
- Keep TikTok content less salesy, more mysterious
- Keep Instagram slightly more polished and product-forward
- Push people to execdrops.com, then measure if traffic converts
- If organic traction remains weak after 2 weeks, test small paid spend only on the best-performing post

### Priority 2: Build the registry
- Add clear email capture language across site
- Make early access feel like joining the registry, not a newsletter
- Prepare welcome, abandoned cart, and post-purchase flows
- Use Drop 001 buyers as the founding cohort for Drop 002

### Priority 3: Tighten economics
- Track units sold, conversion, traffic, content output, and spend weekly
- Keep creative production scrappy and cheap
- Do not spend paid budget until one post clearly outperforms others
- Make Drop 002 only after Drop 001 creates either revenue or audience pull

## Content strategy

### Content pillars
1. The machine speaks
2. Proof of alignment
3. Early adopter registry
4. People arguing with the machine
5. Product as artifact

### Platform behavior
**TikTok**
- Prefer short, strange, low-friction posts
- Avoid obvious ad language early
- Fewer links, fewer hashtags, less polished promo behavior

**Instagram**
- Reels + static mockup posts
- Stronger bio/link behavior
- More product clarity is okay

## Weekly scorecard
Every week, track:
- posts published
- views by platform
- saves/shares/comments
- profile visits
- site visits
- units sold
- revenue
- ad spend
- email signups

## Decision rules
- If a post gets disproportionate views or saves, make 3 variants of that angle
- If site traffic rises but sales do not, improve product page and trust/scarcity copy
- If content gets no reach, change hook before changing product
- If Drop 001 gets traction, start teasing Drop 002 before full sellout

## Q2 outcome targets
Minimum viable success:
- consistent posting cadence
- more than 1 non-founder sale
- functioning email capture
- one repeatable content format that platforms accept

Strong Q2 success:
- 10+ total sales
- 100+ email signups
- 1 to 2 posts with real organic traction
- clear concept for Drop 002 backed by audience signal
