## 1. Print-on-Demand: Printful vs Printify

### Verdict: **Printful** for Drop 001

| Factor | Printful | Printify |
|---|---|---|
| Print quality | ★★★★★ — DTG best-in-class | ★★★★☆ — varies by provider |
| Consistency | Very consistent across runs | Variable (different printers) |
| Shopify integration | Native, seamless | Native, seamless |
| Base cost (heavyweight tee) | ~$18–22 | ~$12–18 (varies) |
| Minimum order | 1 unit (POD) | 1 unit (POD) |
| Branding options | Inside label, pack-ins | Limited |
| Speed | 3–7 days production | 3–5 days (varies by printer) |
| Control | Single provider, predictable | Multi-provider, unpredictable |

**Why Printful for Drop 001:**
- EXEC's entire brand is built on quality signals. Inconsistent print quality is brand poison at launch.
- Printful's inside label and poly mailer branding options let us present a premium unboxing without a warehouse.
- The cost delta (~$4–6/unit) is absorbed in the margin at $58 retail.
- As volume scales (Drop 003+), revisit Printify or negotiate bulk rates with a local print house.

### Future consideration: Hybrid model
- Drops 001–003: Printful (quality lock, no MOQ risk)
- Drop 004+: Source a premium blank (e.g., Heavyweight Boxy from Stanley/Stella or a custom blank), screen print locally with 50–100 unit MOQ. Margin improves dramatically.

## 2. Shopify Plan Recommendation

### **Shopify Basic — $39/month**

**Why Basic, not Starter:**
- Starter ($5/mo) doesn't include an online store — just buy links. We need a storefront that matches the EXEC aesthetic.
- Basic gives full storefront, 2 staff accounts, shipping discounts, and 2% transaction fee via Shopify Payments.

**Why not Shopify ($105/mo) yet:**
- Professional reports and 5 staff accounts are overkill at launch.
- Upgrade to Shopify plan at ~$5K/month revenue when reports become useful.

**Essential apps (free or cheap):**
- Printful — free integration
- Klaviyo — email marketing, free up to 500 contacts (essential for drop-based model)
- TikTok Shopping — free, connect directly to For You page
- Loox — product reviews, $9.99/mo (add after first 20 orders)
- Hotjar — free tier, understand what people actually do on site

**Domain:** execwear.com or wearexec.com — register via Shopify or Namecheap (~$12/yr)

## 3. Pricing Strategy

### Drop 001 — Black Heavyweight Tee

| Line Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Printful base (heavyweight DTG, black) | $20.00 |
| Printful print fee (back graphic) | $2.00 |
| Shopify payment processing (2.9% + $0.30) | $2.00 |
| Packaging / branded mailer | $1.00 |
| **Total COGS** | **$25.00** |
| **Retail Price** | **$58.00** |
| **Gross Margin** | **$33.00 (57%)** |

**Why $58:**
- Palace tees retail $60–80. Brain Dead $65–90. Supreme $48–60.
- $58 is premium-adjacent without being luxury-pretentious for a brand launching with no history.
- First drop pricing anchors the brand. Don't undercut yourself. $40 tees become $40 brands.
- The AI angle is a premium justification. "This is the first garment designed entirely by AI" = $58.

**Pricing ladder for future drops:**
- Drop 001: $58 (tee, establish baseline)
- Drop 002: $65–75 (hoodie or different garment, anchor premium)
- Drop 003: $48 (tee again, but "accessible" feels generous by now)
- Collab or limited: $85–120 (machine collab with another brand/artist)

## 4. Launch Sequence

### Phase 0: Pre-Launch (Now → Drop Date)
**Goal: Build list. Create anticipation.**

- [x] Website live with email capture
- [ ] Instagram account created: @exec.wear or @wearexec
- [ ] TikTok account created: @exec.wear
- [ ] Post teaser content (see TikTok strategy below)
- [ ] Target: 500+ email signups before drop
- [ ] Run 1 TikTok video per day for 14 days pre-launch
- [ ] Seed 5–10 early units to niche streetwear accounts (micro-influencers 5K–50K)

### Phase 1: Drop Day
**Goal: Sell out the first run. Create scarcity.**

- Send email to full list (subject: "The machine is ready.")
- Post on TikTok + Instagram simultaneously at 12PM EST
- First run: **100 units max** — artificial scarcity is real scarcity at launch
- "Sold out" page better than "available" page for brand perception
- Add waitlist immediately on sellout

### Phase 2: Post-Drop (Week 2–4)
**Goal: Amplify. Document the sell-out.**

- Post sell-out content ("001 is closed. 002 is loading.")
- Share unboxing content from buyers (repost everything)
- Start teasing Drop 002 — different product type
- Begin collecting reviews
- Run retargeting to waitlist for Drop 002

### Phase 3: Drop 002 (4–8 weeks post-001)
**Goal: Prove it wasn't a fluke.**

- AI designs something new — different garment category
- Expand email list with every buyer + waitlist
- Target same audience + buyers of 001 who want 002
- Price at $65+

## 5. TikTok Content Strategy — First 30 Days

### Core Content Pillars

**Pillar 1: "The Reveal" (Days 1–3)**
Show the brand existing. Don't explain it. Let it land.
- Video: Black screen. Text appears character by character in monospace: "DESIGNED BY MACHINE." Cut to tee. No music. No voiceover. Let the sound design (keyboard typing SFX) do the work.
- Goal: Curiosity. Comments asking "wait, what is this?"

**Pillar 2: "The Explanation" (Days 4–7)**
Answer the question everyone's asking.
- Video: Screen recording of AI generating the brand concept in real time. Then: "this is the result." Show the tee.
- Hook: "We gave AI full creative control over a clothing brand. Here's what it built."

**Pillar 3: "The Argument" (Days 8–14)**
Debate the premise. Provoke.
- Videos that invite takes: "Is AI-designed fashion legitimate?" — don't give an answer. Let the comments fight.
- The brand's silence on "is this good?" is the point. The machine doesn't need to defend itself.

**Pillar 4: "The Product" (Days 15–21)**
Actually show the thing.
- Honest styling videos — not aspirational lifestyle. Real shots. Dark room. Clean.
- Quality close-ups: fabric, print quality, typography detail.
- The tee should look like it deserves $58 on camera.

**Pillar 5: "The Drop" (Days 22–30)**
Create urgency.
- "Drop 001 goes live [date]. 100 units. First collection ever designed by AI."
- Countdown content daily. Short. Monospace font. Black background. Identical format.
- Day of drop: "It's live." One post. No fluff.

### Posting cadence
- Days 1–14: 1 video/day (build audience)
- Days 15–21: 2 videos/day (acceleration)
- Days 22–30: 2–3 videos/day (drop week)

### Format
- All videos: 9:16 vertical. Black BG where possible. Monospace font overlays.
- Duration: 7–20 seconds. Don't explain. Let people rewatch.
- Audio: Either silence + SFX, or hard-hitting instrumental. No trending sounds — they date content.
- No face on camera — the brand is the machine. No human spokesperson needed.

### Paid amplification (optional, $200 budget)
- Boost best-performing organic video (highest saves/shares) at $10/day for 7 days before drop
- Target: 18–34, US/UK, interests: streetwear, tech, early adopters, fashion
